# Ownership and delivery

**Parent:** [`README.md`](../README.md)

## What this probes

Interviewers want evidence you **drive outcomes** under real constraints: dates, dependencies, and scope-not only that you wrote code. Strong answers show **personal accountability** (what *you* committed to, unblocked, or owned) and a **measurable or decision-based result**.

## Example questions

| # | Question | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Tell me about a time you owned an outcome end-to-end. | Name scope *you* owned vs team. |
| 2 | Hardest deadline you faced; how did you hit it? | Avoid “we hustled” with no *I*. |
| 3 | When did you go above and beyond without being asked? | Must still be credible, not humble-brag. |
| 4 | Describe a project where you were the driving engineer. | Tie to delivery artifact. |
| 5 | Tell me about delivering when upstream dependencies were unreliable. | Good fit for cross-team + ownership combo. |
| 6 | How do you handle “this must ship” pressure? | Tradeoffs explicit. |
| 7 | When did you take responsibility for something outside your job description? | One sentence boundary if never true. |
| 8 | Tell me about a time you committed to a date and kept it. | Numbers help. |
| 9 | What is the most impactful thing you shipped? | Pick one; avoid résumé dump. |
| 10 | When did you fix a problem no one else wanted to own? | On-call / ops stories often fit. |
| 11 | Tell me about juggling multiple deliverables; what did you ship first and why? | Links to prioritization domain too. |
| 12 | How do you communicate risk when a date is at risk? | Process + one example. |

## Gaps and detail drills

- **Drill:** one **date or window** per story, what slipped vs what held, and **one quant** (latency, tickets, adoption).
- [2017 Algar](../work-experience/2017-algar-telecom.md): thin behavioral sections—do not force a hero STAR; point interviewers to later roles if asked for “early ownership.”
