# Customer, user, and trust

**Parent:** [`README.md`](../README.md)

## What this probes

Who **felt** the outcome: external users, internal customers, sellers? Can you connect engineering choices to **pain removed** or **trust preserved**? Some prompts (trust break) have a **high honesty bar**—do not invent.

## Example questions

| # | Question | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Who was the user or customer for your work? | Internal vs external. |
| 2 | Tell me about a seller-facing or customer-facing impact. | Invoice / public SDK users. |
| 3 | When did you discover users were confused, blocked, or failing? | Telemetry, support, dogfood. |
| 4 | Tell me about failing a customer or losing trust. | **Weak if invented**—see gaps. |
| 5 | How do you gather or use user feedback in engineering decisions? | Lightweight process OK. |
| 6 | Internal customers vs external—how does your approach differ? | Principled answer. |
| 7 | When did UX or clarity matter for a technical decision you made? | Profile page / previews. |
| 8 | How do you balance user needs and engineering cost? | Explicit tradeoff. |
| 9 | Tell me about metrics that reflected user happiness or task success. | Define metric + movement. |
| 10 | When did you prioritize reducing recurring pain for operators or sellers? | On-call / invoices. |
| 11 | Tell me about a time expectations were mis-set with a customer or partner. | Recovery narrative. |
| 12 | How do you think about backward compatibility for public API users? | SDK stability story. |

## Gaps and detail drills

- **Medium–weak for “trust break”:** prep already flags **no crisp trust-break STAR**—closest is on-call pain or SDK stability; **mine invoice story for user impact** (what “bad” looked like, segment, volume) or use a **short boundary** + redirect.
- **Drill:** add remembered detail (tickets, seller segment, SLA) or admit limits—better than confident invention.
