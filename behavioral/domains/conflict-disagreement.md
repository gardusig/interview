# Conflict and disagreement

**Parent:** [`README.md`](../README.md)

## What this probes

Can you **disagree constructively**: use data, respect roles, commit after decision, and avoid sounding bitter? Especially for **manager** disagreement, frame **loyalty + evidence + execution** (see [sensitive-prompts](sensitive-prompts.md)).

## Example questions

| # | Question | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Tell me about a technical disagreement with a peer. | Opposing view in one sentence. |
| 2 | Disagreement with PM on scope or priority. | Respect + outcome. |
| 3 | Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. | IC-safe framing; see sensitive doc. |
| 4 | When did you win someone over with data? | What metric convinced them. |
| 5 | When did you **lose** an argument but still execute well? | Maturity signal. |
| 6 | Describe pushback on a design or API. | Tradeoffs, not ego. |
| 7 | Tell me about conflict in code review. | Behavior, not personality. |
| 8 | When did two teams want opposite things? | How you brokered or escalated. |
| 9 | How do you disagree without being disagreeable? | Principles + example. |
| 10 | When did you escalate a disagreement? | When local resolution failed. |
| 11 | Tell me about advocating for quality when others wanted speed. | Often overlaps load-testing gate. |
| 12 | How do you handle repeated pushback on the same issue? | Know when to stop re-litigating. |

## Gaps and detail drills

- **Drill:** for Python concurrency story, state the **opposing design** in one line and **how you measured** the outcome.
- Manager-disagreement variant: rehearse **same** stories framed as “raised risk with evidence; once decided, shipped”—or honestly **no** instance + peer disagreement.
