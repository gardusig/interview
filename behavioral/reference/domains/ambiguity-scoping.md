# Ambiguity and scoping

**Parent:** [`README.md`](../README.md)

## What this probes

Can you **reduce fuzziness**: clarify success, shrink unknowns, align owners, and still ship? Interviewers listen for **how you drove clarity** when specs or ownership were missing—not passive waiting.

## Example questions

| # | Question | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Tell me about a poorly specified project you clarified. | Your actions to narrow scope. |
| 2 | When did requirements keep changing mid-flight? | How you re-scoped or time-boxed. |
| 3 | Describe unclear ownership; how did you proceed? | Escalation + options, not blame. |
| 4 | Tell me about ambiguity where you were **not** the decision-maker. | Emphasize what **you** controlled. |
| 5 | When did you turn a fuzzy problem into something deliverable? | Milestones, spikes. |
| 6 | How do you handle “figure it out” assignments? | Process + one story. |
| 7 | Tell me about discovering hidden constraints late. | Detection + mitigation. |
| 8 | When did you write the spec because none existed? | Artifact + review. |
| 9 | How do you align multiple teams on what “done” means? | Concrete sync or doc. |
| 10 | When did you time-box exploration? | Stop condition matters. |
| 11 | Tell me about a project where the goal was unclear for weeks. | Honest if messy. |
| 12 | How do you prevent scope creep on your own work? | Personal discipline story. |
